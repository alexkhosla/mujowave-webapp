The Android sailing instrument app

GPS speed over ground, compass heading, heel and trim — readable in sunlight, at a distance, from the wrong end of the boat.

Speedometer

An expanded 0–6 kn easy to read scale, big digital readout, plus max, 30-second average and trend.

Marine-style heading tape

Magnetic or true at the tap of a button, with a rotating compass tape like the real instrument.

Heel and trim at a glance

Your boat from astern, with a green target zone, capsize warning marks and a sliding trim indicator.

Private by design

Works completely offline. Your position never leaves your phone. No accounts, no ads, no tracking.

Onboard hardware in development

Rugged Bluetooth devices for the boat itself, designed to pair with the app.

Smart lantern

Bluetooth-controlled onboard light with battery telemetry and runtime estimates.

Motion & environment sensing

Fixed onboard sensors for attitude, pressure and temperature — mounted to the hull, not your pocket.

Managed from the app

Pairing, control and over-the-air firmware updates, all from the same app.

Join the beta

The app is in closed beta — Google requires 12 testers for 14 days before public release. Joining is self-service and takes about two minutes. Use the same Google account for both steps — the one signed into Google Play on your Android phone.

  1. Join the tester group. Open the Mujowave beta group and tap Join group. This just authorises your account — no emails, no discussion list.
  2. Become a tester. Open the beta page on your phone and tap Become a tester.
  3. Install from Google Play — and keep it installed for a couple of weeks. Going sailing with it is the whole idea.
